5S Training Module

Establishing workplace efficiency in a growing production department of a midsize company through an interactive 5S training module

UX Researcher · Web Developer

Skills: user research, user testing, persona mapping, information architecture, html, css, javascript, visual design

Overview

The Problem

As companies expand in reach, so does their product line. In return, their production departments should improve and grow to meet increasing demand. This should be the case, however, Epiq Solutions, a Telecommunications company had been struggling with this. While their product demand continued to increase, the production department stayed stagnant and struggled to meet deadlines. Through increased observation, the cause seems to be an outdated and disorganized work process. While there is an attempt at an organized work process, it is forgotten by production employees.

Goals

  • Improve production efficiency by reducing time wasted searching for tools and materials.

  • Implement standardization of processes and workplace organization.

  • Help production employees easily understand and apply the 5S system in their daily routines.

Solutions

I created an interactive training module that guides the production employees through the 5 phases of 5S: Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardize and Sustain. The training module includes videos lessons, module quizzes, progress dashboard and reviews to establish and refresh production employees' 5S mindset.

Research

Observation

In order to establish an effective solution to Epiq’s production issue, I wanted to find the root cause of Epiq’s disorganization.

To do so, I immersed myself into the environment as if I were an employee myself. I assembled and tested products using tools and materials and following the instructions.

It was then when the issue was clear to me. The production department had a severe lack of organization and 5S. Because I was not given clear instructions of where to put tools and materials, I found myself tossing them aside or placing them in random places, making it difficult for the next person to find what they are looking for.

In addition to my observations, I gathered that

4 out of 5 production employees were older with limited computer experience.

Several employees were not highly proficient in English, which created additional challenges in understanding written instructions.

Interviews

To follow up my observations, I interviewed some of the employees in the production department, and what I gathered from them confirmed my suspicions.

Questions and Answers

What is the most challenging part of your work process?

“ Other than the assembling of parts, finding the tools and equipment needed. Sometimes if I need a specific screwdriver, I’ll have to look around for it.”

When you are going about your workday, what things do you notice and what do you wish were different?

“The workbenches tend to get very messy with tools and material, and I wish there were a system to keep things clean.”

“There just isn’t an instruction manual, so if I forget where something goes I don’t have anything to refer back to.”

Has production ever attempted to establish an organization system?

“Yes, but it always reverts back to normal after a bit. I think people just forget to follow it because the system isn’t really pushed.”

Personas

Ideation

Brainstorm

I generated multiple ideas for improving efficiency, ranging from a printed instruction manual to a digital tool that employees could reference daily. The key was to find a solution that wouldn’t just introduce new rules but would actively engage employees and reinforce good habits.

Priorities

I wanted to focus on making sure my solution was engaging, feasible and simple. A digital training module stood out because it could be updated easily, standardized across teams, and used as both onboarding and refresher material.

Sketches

along with site mapping, I developed some rough sketches to give me a rough idea of where I wanted to place each element of the course before I started coding.

Testing and Insights

Testing

To test the training module, I had the employees try out a draft of what would be the final website.

As the production employees navigated the training module site, I gave them specific tasks to follow.

  • Complete the first module and quiz.

  • Find the module review pages.

  • provide real time feedback.
    •

Findings

Employees appreciated the structure but requested clearer quiz questions.

Employees found the website very intuitive and easy to use.

They wanted an feature that would make it easier to find the review pages.

Tweaks

Based on feedback, I rewrote confusing quiz questions and introduced a feature: in the completed section of the overview page, a button next to the completed module will take you to that module review page.

These changes reduced user errors and increased satisfaction during testing.

Reflection

Research impact

By embedding myself in the production process, I uncovered the true pain point—lack of organizational consistency—not simply “slow workers.

Design Insight

The project reinforced that training isn’t just about delivering information; it’s about creating habits through repeated, engaging interactions.

Professional Growth

I gained hands-on experience combining UX research with front-end development, bridging research insights directly into a working product.

Next Steps

If I revisited the project, I’d explore adding gamification elements

e.g., badges for completing modules to further motivate long-term adoption of the 5S system.
